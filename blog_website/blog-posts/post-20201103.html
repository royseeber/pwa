Should You Give Bootstrap The Boot?

If you're like me, you would have come across frameworks like Bootstrap while trying to figure out the nuances of responsive design. You probably sat down over a weekend and began playing around with container, row and column classes unable to contain your excitement as columns began automatically appearing and disappearing as you resized your screen. With the advent of HTML 5 and the CSS Grid component this has now become child's play.

Final Thoughts

If you want to experiment with these ideas on your own, make sure you have the following meta tag in the head section of your html page. <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">

Hopefully you will now feel inspired to implement your own responsive web designs without relying on third party frameworks. In the era of Progressive Web Applications where performance is so vital; you would do well to streamline your apps by disposing of those monolithic frameworks that keep you trapped inside another persons head. Say goodbye to Bootstrap, Materialize, JQuery , React, Angular and all the other bloatware. Say hello to becoming your own designer using CSS.
